DAKSH Training Simulator

Unity · PC Simulation · Serial/USB Input · Training Controls

  • Worked as the main Unity programmer on a PC-based operational training simulator.
  • Integrated actual remote-control hardware input through Serial/USB communication.
  • Built simulated machine/robot-style movement, dashboard UI, cameras, feedback, warnings, and deployment support.
View Case Study

Context

PC-based operational training simulator developed for a DRDO India-related training context. The project was coordinated through a mediator/team that managed project design and presentation.

My Role

Main Unity Programmer. I handled around 90% of the Unity-side implementation, including programming execution, simulator behavior, UI/dashboard logic, control integration, deployment, and post-deployment support changes.

Tech Stack

Unity, C#, PC build, UGUI, Serial/USB communication, keyboard/mouse input, joystick/button hardware input, custom scripts, camera systems, animation, physics/collision response, and 3D model setup.

What I Built

  • Bridge between Unity and actual physical remote-control hardware with multiple joysticks and buttons.
  • Machine/robot-style movement simulation, arm/part movement, camera switching, multiple camera angles, and operation feedback.
  • Dashboard UI, status/warning indicators, training procedure flow, scene setup, animation states, physics/collision response, build preparation, deployment, and support updates.

Important Clarification

The simulator used the actual training remote-control hardware for input, but it did not control the real machine. Unity visualized the simulated machine/robot-style response on screen for training purposes.

Outcome

Working PC simulator delivered to the mediator/client/team, used for training/demo and stakeholder presentation, with additional support provided after deployment.
